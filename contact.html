â“ Frequently Asked Questions

How can I collaborate with your research team? +
We welcome research collaborations with academic institutions, mental health organizations, and individual researchers. Contact us at neintosick96@gmail.com with details about your research interests and proposed collaboration areas. We're particularly interested in cross-cultural studies, validation research, and longitudinal outcome studies.
Are you open to international partnerships? +
Absolutely! We actively seek international partnerships for global expansion, cultural adaptation research, and cross-border mental health technology development. Our vision is to serve university students worldwide, and international partnerships are essential to achieving this goal.
Can I access your research data or publications? +
Our research findings are being prepared for publication in peer-reviewed journals and conference proceedings. Once published, our work will be freely accessible. For collaboration purposes, we can share appropriate datasets and methodologies under proper research agreements.
How can students get involved with your project? +
We welcome student involvement through research internships, thesis projects, and collaborative studies. Graduate students in psychology, computer science, and related fields can contact us to discuss potential opportunities for involvement in our research and development activities.
What makes your approach different from other mental health apps? +
Our approach is distinguished by extensive evidence-based development (300+ expert interviews), continuous validation by mental health professionals, and specific focus on university students aged 20+. We prioritize clinical appropriateness, cultural sensitivity, and measurable outcomes over generic wellness features.
When will the Bandi app be available for download? +
We're currently in the final development and testing phase. Beta testing with Korean university students will begin in Q1 2025, with broader availability planned for Q3-Q4 2025. International expansion timeline depends on partnership development and cultural adaptation research.
