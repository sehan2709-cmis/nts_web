🔬 Research Methodology

Our evidence-based approach to developing mental wellness technology through rigorous research and expert validation

🎯 Research Overview

The Nein to Sick team believes that effective mental wellness technology must be grounded in solid research and validated by mental health professionals. Our approach to developing the Bandi app was built on a foundation of over 300 in-depth interviews, continuous expert consultation, and iterative validation processes.

This comprehensive research methodology ensures that every feature in the Bandi app is not only user-friendly but also psychologically sound and clinically relevant for our target audience of university students.

📋 Research Process Timeline

Phase 1: Problem Identification
Initial research into mental health challenges facing university students in South Korea, identifying key pain points and unmet needs in current support systems.
Phase 2: Expert Consultation
In-depth interviews with 8 professional counselors and 3 graduate students to understand best practices in mental health support and digital intervention design.
Phase 3: User Experience Research
Comprehensive interviews with 150 individuals with lived experience of depression and anxiety treatment to understand user needs, preferences, and barriers.
Phase 4: Feature Development & Validation
Iterative design and testing of app features with continuous feedback from mental health professionals and target users.
Phase 5: Expert Review & Refinement
Final validation of the complete app experience by our panel of mental health experts to ensure clinical appropriateness and safety.

🔬 Research Principles

Evidence-Based Design
Every feature is grounded in psychological research and validated therapeutic approaches
User-Centered Approach
Extensive user research ensures the app meets real needs and preferences of our target audience
Expert Validation
Continuous involvement of mental health professionals throughout the development process
Ethical Considerations
Careful attention to privacy, safety, and appropriate boundaries in digital mental health support
Cultural Sensitivity
Understanding of Korean cultural context while designing for potential global expansion
Iterative Improvement
Ongoing research and refinement based on user feedback and emerging best practices
